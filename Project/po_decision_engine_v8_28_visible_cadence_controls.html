How Smart ROP is calculated
×

Smart ROP keeps the statistical ROP structure but makes D̄ transparent and selectable — which treats every item the same and ignores variability — with a statistically grounded formula that uses your real demand pattern and real lead-time history.

ROP = Cycle stock + Review buffer + Safety stock

Cycle  = D̄ × LT̄
Review = D̄ × R
Safety = z × √((LT̄ + R)·σ_d² + D̄²·σ_LT²)

R = your review cadence, read from the ReviewDays column when present (default 7 days). The safety term combines demand and lead-time variability over the full lead-time-plus-review exposure window.

Where the numbers come from

  • LT̄ (lead time) — uses the real PO→Receipt history from the Lead_Time_History sheet when loaded (median per vendor, computed from your actual goods receipts). Falls back to SAP's LeadTimeDays column only when no history is available.
  • σ_LT (lead-time variance) — computed from the same real history. When unavailable, defaults to 30% of mean lead time.
  • D̄ (daily demand) — selected from item-level demand evidence in the uploaded file. Default mode is Conservative evidence median across positive signals: DailyDemand, AvgMonthly3M/30.4, AvgMonthly12M/30.4, and AvgMonthlySamePeriodLY/30.4. The user can switch to SQL DailyDemand, 3M, 12M, or seasonal explicitly.
  • σ_d (demand variance) — CV computed across your 3M, 12M and same-period-last-year monthly readings (clamped to the range [0.05, 3.0] to keep safety stock finite when a SKU has only 2–3 noisy readings).

PO eligibility & guardrails (v8.22)

A SKU produces an authorised PO quantity only when it is at or near its reorder point (inventory position ≤ ROP × 1.15 — the same threshold that drives the Buy now / Buy soon labels). An item that is above its reorder point but below its order-up-to target shows a target-fill suggestion in its detail view, but it is not auto-added to the PO and is not counted in PO totals — so the Buy-Intelligence panel, Auto-add, the basket and the export always agree on one number.

Visible PO cadence controls (v8.28): Kicker, Garmin and JL default to cadence-buy logic, and the buyer can now switch the planning window to Weekly, every 2 weeks, Monthly, a specific next PO date, or custom trigger/target/max cover days. ROP remains visible for risk, but the PO-authoritative quantity follows the selected cadence. In Auto mode the uploaded Need column wins only for Weekly planning; Monthly and Custom calculate from the selected cadence unless you choose Always use uploaded Need.

The engine returns zero (and routes the line to Review) when any of these hold: no demand evidence (D̄ ≤ 0), a negative inventory position with no demand (an inventory/backorder reconciliation issue, not a purchase), a missing or zero unit cost, a missing vendor, a run-out/sunset lifecycle, or non-moving (FSN = N) status.

What each piece means

  • D̄ — selected demand basis from the uploaded item-level fields; it is not forced to a 60/40 blend and does not use vendor totals.
  • LT̄ — average lead time. Uses real OPOR→OPDN history from the Lead-Time sheet if loaded, otherwise SAP's LeadTimeDays
  • σ_d — demand variability = CV × D̄, where the CV is computed across your 3M / 12M / same-period-LY readings and clamped to [0.05, 3.0]
  • σ_LT — lead-time variability from real PO history; falls back to 30% of LT̄ when unknown
  • z — service-level multiplier from the ABC×FSN matrix below

Service-level matrix (z-scores)

Tier Description Service level z
A-FHigh value, fast moving — cash cows97.5%1.96

Why this is better than the old method

  • A-Fast items get MORE protection — the old method ignored lead-time variance entirely. Brands like Kicker have lead times of 17 ± 21 days — that variance alone justifies extra safety stock.
  • C-Slow and Non-moving items get LESS — no point hoarding stock that doesn't move. Safety stock = 0 for N-class items.
  • You can see exactly why — click any item row to expand and see the ROP breakdown: cycle / safety / review components plus the z, σ_d, σ_LT values that produced them.

Toggle "SAP legacy" in the banner if you want to A/B-compare against the original numbers, or before fully committing to the new method.

v8.5 anti-overbuy guardrails

Three protections were added to prevent overbuying — verified against your real GEMSEN data:

  • MOQ overshoot guard — if a vendor's MOQ would force you to take ≥180 days of cover on a non-A item (≥365 for A-class, ≥540 for A-Fast), the auto-recommendation is suppressed and the item is sent to Manual review instead. Stops a single $5,000 unit purchase becoming a 500-day cover bet just because MOQ=1.
  • Tightened lifecycle detection — 365+ days no sale is now flagged as Run-out even if the 12-month average has decayed to zero, as long as there's any historical evidence (prior demand, seasonal pattern, active months, or on-hand stock). Also, 180+ days no sale + 75% drop in 3M vs 12M now triggers Run-out (auto).
  • Cover uses inventory position consistently — the days-of-cover number you see is computed from OnHand + OnOrder − Committed, the same number the engine compares against ROP. No more mismatched numerators between the visible cover and the underlying decision.

Audited against your real data: v8.5 eliminates 100% of items that would have ended up with >180 days of cover post-PO under v8.4 logic. 832 additional dormant items are now correctly flagged as Run-out. PO recommendation $-value tightened by ≈8%.

How the Decision Score is calculated
×

Score range: 0 – 100. Higher score = more reason to order this item now. The score is a sum of six weighted components:

ComponentRuleMax pts
Stock cover urgencyCover < lead+review days → 30 · <60d → 20 · <90d → 10 · else 030
ROP gapmin(25, round(ropGap / ROP × 25))25
ABC × XYZ × FSNABC and FSN priority plus XYZ forecastability: A/F and stable items receive higher urgency; non-moving or discontinued items receive 015
Gross marginround(margin × 10) — so 40% margin → 4 pts, 100% → 10 pts10
SeasonalitySeasonal Uplift → 10 · New/Recent → 7 · Stable → 5 · else 010
Velocity (months active)≥10mo → 10 · ≥6mo → 6 · ≥3mo → 3 · else 010
Total possible100

Hard zero: items with no demand in the last 12 months, or no sale for >180 days, score 0 regardless of the other components. These show as "Skip" in the Decision column.

Click any row in the table to expand it — the row detail shows each component's contribution for that specific item, so you can see exactly why it scored what it did.

Quick start
×

1 · Load your data

One .xlsx file with up to 4 sheets is the easiest way (see fetch_po_data.py). The engine auto-routes sheets by name:

  • PO_Recommendation — main items table (required)
  • Brand_Sales_GP — switches Brand Health from proxy → actual
  • Lead_Time_History — replaces 30d default with real PO→receipt averages
  • Purchase_History — populates the year-over-year purchases tab

Alternative: paste from SSMS results grid (Ctrl+Shift+C copies with headers), or upload individual CSVs.

2 · Filter to your scope

Use the search box, vendor/brand dropdowns, or click any chip (Below ROP, Cover < lead time, Fast/Slow/Non-moving, Dead, Obsolete, …) to focus on a decision context.

3 · Build the PO basket

Tick items, edit Order qty inline. The Score column ranks urgency 0-100 — click the ⓘ next to the Score header to see the formula. When ready, click Generate PO ↓ to preview grouped by vendor, then export CSV or copy lines.

4 · Brand Health (header toggle)

Inventory Turnover, DSI, Inv-to-Sales, GP%, FSN distribution and obsolete value per brand — plus a quadrant chart. Without the brand/lead-time sheets, numbers are proxy estimates; with them, accounting-grade actuals.

5 · Multi-currency

"Vendor currency" in the header flips any vendor to CAD. PO previews then quote that vendor in CAD with USD-equivalent below; CSV export adds Currency / FxRate columns.

Tip: ☆ Click any column header to sort · ☆ Click any row to expand · ☆ Click any brand in Brand Health to drill into its items.

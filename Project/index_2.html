03 — PO cadence

Risk detection ≠ purchase execution.

ROP stays visible as a risk signal, but order quantity follows your buying cycle — not a static long-horizon fill target. An item can be risky without generating a cash-heavy PO.

CADENCE CONTROLS
Planning window
Weekly / biweekly / monthly / custom — matches your real buying rhythm
Review days
Days until next planned PO cycle — scopes the current decision window
Order trigger cover
Buy only when cover is at or below this threshold — prevents early buying
Top-up target cover
Qty = demand × target cover minus current position — keeps order size practical
Max cover after PO
MOQ orders above this go to Review — protects working capital from overbuying
Apply cadence to
All vendors/items or a specific scope — adds vendor-level buying discipline
04 — Brand health

Which brands are healthy? Which ones are trapping capital?

Brand Health connects purchasing decisions to vendor-level performance. The risk matrix plots movement strength vs capital trapped in one view.

BRAND-LEVEL HEALTH
Inventory Health Metrics

Five signals. One vendor table.

Sortable by any metric. Click any brand to drill into its items. The totals row and FSN share % let you benchmark the whole portfolio at a glance.

  • Inventory turnover ratio — Standard 2–4×. Red = critical, amber = watchable, green = healthy
  • Days Sales of Inventory (DSI) — Standard 90–180 days
  • Inv-to-sales ratio — capital invested to make $1 of sales. Standard 0.167–0.25
  • FSN classification — Fast / Slow / Non-moving stock value by brand (F > 80%, N < 10%)
  • Obsolete inventory — not sold for 361+ days. Write-off candidates
BRAND RISK MATRIX
Capital At Risk

Movement risk vs capital trapped — in one scatter.

More useful than a treemap: the bubble chart separates healthy capital from slow and obsolete exposure. Bubble size = stock value. X = turnover strength. Y = trapped capital share.

  • Healthy brands cluster bottom-right (high movement, low trapped capital)
  • Watch brands in the yellow zone — review slow or imbalanced capital
  • Critical brands flagged at top-left — immediate vendor review action
  • Capital at risk strip: Fast / Slow / Non-moving / Obsolete in one stacked bar
  • Click any bubble to drill into item-level breakdown for that vendor
05 — Lost sales intelligence

Confirmed losses vs preventable risk. Evidence-based.

Every figure is traceable to a row. No modelled numbers. Lost sales split into operational vs buying root cause.

LOST SALES — A1 & M3
Revenue at Risk

Two tiers. Two actions. Zero guessing.

A1 confirmed losses are past-due back-orders — already lost revenue split into fulfillment-fail vs real stockout. M3 preventable losses are items below ROP that will become lost sales if the next PO doesn't ship in time.

  • A1 — 100% proof. Past-due back-orders already on the books
  • M3 — ~90% confidence. Below reorder point. Action: PO this week
  • Upload weekly stock sheet to split A1 into fulfillment-fail vs real stockout
  • Formula visible inline: M3 = Σ max(0, ROP − (OnHand + OnOrder − Committed)) × VendorPriceUSD
MONTHLY VIEW + BRAND BREAKDOWN
Brand-Level Exposure

Which brands. Which SKUs. How much.

Monthly bar chart shows projected preventable revenue at risk by period. Brand breakdown table shows confirmed A1 vs preventable M3 exposure — expandable to individual SKU level.

  • Past = A1 confirmed by ship date. Forward = M3 projected by daily demand
  • By tier and by month views — switch between them instantly
  • Expand any brand row to see item-level confirmed and preventable exposure
  • Share % shows which brands own the largest revenue risk
